Are Rising Home Prices Forcing Californians Out?

This is the third in a series of articles examining issues related to housing affordability.



Census 5-yr migration data (2013-2017) and IRS migration data (1990-2017). More details can be found in this project's Github repository.

Qs: - *Are* they leaving (show data since 2011)? - Where are they going? - Are home prices the culprit? (are [relative] CA home prices increasing- esp. compared to states/counties where they moved?) -

*Source: IRS tax filing data (adjusted to match annual CA population figures)


To track domestic migration in and out of California, I examined IRS tax filing data from 1990 to 2017. The following findings became clearly apparent: First, the narrative that California perpetually experiences greater outflow of residents than arrivals from other states is firmly supported. In the fiscal years where data was available (1990-2017), California's net migration figures ranged from -16,000 to -460,000 migrants. Second, net migration appears to ebb and flow, with 3 apparent peaks in the chart to the left. Digging deeper into the differences between peak and non-peak years might reveal the specific factors that impact migration. In my analysis below, I attempt to distill relevant factors by comparing housing and income data of destination counties in peak years versus non-peak years.



*Note: international migration data is excluded
