Examining IRS tax return data from 1990 to 2017 reveals a few interesting patterns. First, it confirms the narrative that California has experienced greater outflow of residents than arrivals from other states. Second, this net negative migration appears to ebb and flow, with 3 apparent peaks in the chart to the left. Perhaps comparing demographic data of destination counties in peak years to non-peak years will reveal some potential causes behind California's net negative migration.
